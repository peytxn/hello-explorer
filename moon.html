Journey Through Space

Moon

Earth's Cosmic Companion

The Moon is Earth’s closest neighbor in space and its constant companion. It doesn’t have air to breathe or weather to watch, but its rocky surface is packed with craters from ancient space impacts. The Moon lights up our night sky by reflecting sunlight and plays a big role in creating ocean tides on Earth. As it circles our planet, the Moon changes shape—from full and glowing to a thin crescent—reminding us that space is always in motion.
